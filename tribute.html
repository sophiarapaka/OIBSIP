The Boy from Rameswaram Who Ignited a Nation

From selling newspapers at dawn to guiding India's most powerful rockets skyward — Kalam's life was the greatest proof that dreams do not discriminate.

A tribute  ·  OIBSIP Web Development Internship

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam grew up in a small island town, walking miles to school and rising before sunrise to earn money for his family. There was nothing in his early circumstances that guaranteed greatness — except the unrelenting fire inside him.


He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he led the team that put India's first indigenous satellite, Rohini, into orbit in 1980. It was the beginning of a legacy in rocketry and missile development that would define India's strategic power for decades.


When he became President in 2002, he brought the same simplicity and curiosity of a scientist to the highest office. He met thousands of students, wrote poetry, played the veena, and never once lost touch with the young. He called them "ignited minds" — the most powerful resource on earth.

Life & Milestones
1931
Born in Rameswaram
Born into a Tamil Muslim family. Sold newspapers to fund his own schooling.
1958
Joins DRDO
Begins career as a scientist, working on India's first indigenous hovercraft.
1969
Moves to ISRO
Joins ISRO and leads the SLV-III project — India's first satellite launch vehicle.
1980
Rohini in Orbit
Successfully places Rohini satellite in orbit, making India a space-faring nation.
1992
IGMDP Success
Leads the missile development programme producing Agni, Prithvi, Akash & more.
1998
Pokhran-II
Key figure in India's second nuclear tests, establishing nuclear deterrence.
2002
11th President
Elected President of India — the "People's President," beloved by students.
2015
Final Lecture
Passed away mid-lecture at IIM Shillong — inspiring students until his last breath.
